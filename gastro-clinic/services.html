Therapeutic Endoscopic

Endoscopy involves using a telescope with instruments that is inserted through the mouth or anus (upper or lower GI) in order to monitor and observe internal disorders or perform certain procedures that includes eliminating polyps, to stop internal bleeding, broadening narrow portions of the GI tract, or to carry out biopsies for cancer. Specialised consultants at Gastro Clinics are highly qualified, certified and trained to perform all these procedures cautiously, making it easier for gastroenterologists to examine the kind of disease that is prevalent and determine a treatment that is necessary.

Flexible Sigmoidoscopy & Colonoscopy

Flexible sigmoidoscopy is used to examine and evaluate the lower part of the large intestine (colon) via a thin, flexible tube that is inserted into the rectum and most of the sigmoid colon. The procedure is performed to investigate intestinal signs and symptoms and explore possible causes of abdominal pain, chronic diarrhea, rectal bleeding, changes in bowel habits and other intestinal problems, along with signs of cancers or polyps. Whereas, a colonoscopy examines and tests the entire colon, particularly when a sigmoidoscopy cannot diagnose the entire colon.

Polypectomy

Polypectomy is a minimally invasive procedure that is used by the doctors to remove abnormal growth of tissues from inside the colon (large intestine), known as polyps. It is usually discovered as part of a routine colonoscopy and a therapeutic procedure in order to examine whether the polyps is cancerous, precancerous, or non-cancerous. However, polyps may also be assessed via a non-invasive imaging test such as a CT scan, ultrasound, or a magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scan, in cases of evaluating the causes of weight loss, bleeding, or other symptoms.
