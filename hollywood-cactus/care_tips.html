CARE AND TIPS

Here is how you can nurture your Cactus plants

While, both cacti and succulents are excellent plants to have in your home and some of the easiest to maintain; there are a few things you will need to consider before getting started. To help cacti and succulents live longer and healthier lives, we bring to you the best tips you will ever need!

Watering your plants:

It is best to water your cacti and succulents, whenever the compost dries out fully, after your last watering. If you have placed the plants in a colder and shady place, then it is better to give them less water. Plus, in winter times, both cacti and succulents need less water and they should only be given enough to keep the soil almost dry. It should be appreciated that, cacti only need a limited amount of water to prevent them from shriveling; humidity on the other hand can be a killer. If you are planning to position your plant somewhere humid, like your steamy bathroom, you should go for a rainforest plant, such as Jungle Cacti. Overwatering can kill cacti and succulents because they are desert plants that need their soil to be dry.

Allow your plants sunlight

All cacti and succulents love sitting in a sunny window, all day long. In the summer, your patio would be an ideal place for them but you need to understand that the more sun they get, the more water they will need.

Using a fertilizer

Regardless of where you put your cacti and succulents; out in the open, where they are exposed to seasonal extremes or indoors, in a sunny spot; fertilizers, can help them grow happily. Similar to how it works for any other plant, fertilizing helps cactus plants adapt and grow. For this purpose, you can use any good houseplant food, with higher concentration of Potassium and phosphorus than nitrogen.

Take it easy

Other than heavy-handed watering and feeding, these plants can withstand most maltreatment and are very low in maintenance. Since they thrive on neglect, they can be perfect room-mates; all you have to do is check on them every now and then, and perhaps water them twice a month.
